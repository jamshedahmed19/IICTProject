5 Of The Best New Restaurants in Lahore


05 Oct 2020 By Sinwan Maryam Zahid

The city of Lahore prioritizes comfort. When we like something, we hold on to it. While this isn't bad, it isn't too great either as restaurants tend to get more laid-back and repeat a lot of what's already circulating. This paves the way for an unhealthy trend in the food industry, with everyone offering the same dishes, leaving little room for innovation and experimentation. This changed last year when Awesamosas, SUMO, Bovinoes, and Thanda Garam graced us with their presence. In 2018, even more restaurateurs have picked up on this trend and have given us some exciting new menus, cuisines and themes to cherish.

1.Urban Kitchen

The restaurant came to Gulberg’s College Road earlier this year with its contemporary, edgy artworks hung over corrugated tin walls, including black-and-white pictures of popular moments from Pakistan’s past and trendy neon signs. The restaurant was a break from boring interiors and also miles away from mind-numbing menus with a variety of comfort foods on the menu. They wowed us with their Oishii Katsu Curry, Parisian Apple Crumble Tart and Peanut Butter Cheesecake and further surprised us by adding many exciting options to try out.

2.NOVU

One of the restaurants to truly revive DHA Y Block’s Terrace Avenue market earlier this year was NOVU. While small and compact, the restaurant can accommodate up to 25 people, indoors and outdoors combined. The reason why NOVU made it on this list is purely because of its consistency in taste and decent pricing. The menu may be more Chinese than Thai, but that doesn’t take away from the fact that one of the best Crunchy Honey Beef bowls can be found here.

3.A Piece of Cake

A small, blue bakery opened up in DHA's X Block just shortly after Rina's Kitchenette closed down a few shops down this April, and people haven't since been able to stop talking about A Piece of Cake or the two superwomen running it. Owners Hafsa and Anum serve some of the yummiest malt cupcakes and salted caramel brownies in the city with their own personal touch. The experience at the bakery cafe is more intimate and the friendly duo open up to customers about their experience as females running a restaurant and how challenging it can get sometimes.

4.CheWon - Korean Restaurant

A bit pricey, but how many Korean restaurants can you name in the city? Previously Udon House and now rebranded as CheWon, the restaurant is spacious and airy, with a Korean couple serving some of the most authentic Korean dishes you can find in the country. Prepare to nibble on some sour and acidic Kimchi with your metal chopsticks, which again is a very Korean touch. The eatery is not over-the-top in terms of aesthetics but prepare to spend an average of Rs1,500 regardless.

5.Pasta La Vista

Towards the end of the year Pasta La Vista made an entrance in Lahore in a quiet, dark corner of a forever busy road. The restaurant chose to be hidden away to keep its occupancy limited. It wasn't just that but the entire restaurant was centered around love for one ingredient only: PASTA. Fresh, handmade pasta has never been the norm with existing restaurants but at Pasta La Vista everything is prepared from scratch. From meatball spaghetti to cheesy, fluffy gnocchi, it's all on the menu at the new eatery. If you have an undying appetite for pasta, value money, like a groovy playlist and are up to try a delicious New York Cheesecake, then this is the place for you.
